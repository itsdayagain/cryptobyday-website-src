## The Posemesh: Collaborative Spatial Computing

So, what makes Auki interesting?

In three words: **collaborative spatial computing**.

Let's unpack that.

Collaborative spacial computing is the core technology that will connect machine
intelligence to the physical world, and it's going to be an enormous market.

At the heart of this is Auki's *posemesh* protocol, a digital 3D map of physical
space. Think of it as the digital twin of the analog world, which lets machines
understand everything that's going on around them, so that they can navigate,
observe, analyze, and collaborate. This is the missing piece that AI and
intelligent robots need in order to step out into the real world and actually
make our everyday lives easier.

There are so many ways this technology could be applied. For example it's
already being used to help supermarkets track inventory, to guide visitors
around events, and for augmented-reality games. But this is only the tiny tiny
beginning — it's still so early that most use cases haven't even been discovered
yet. 

I know, ChatGPT is getting all the hype right now. But making AI understand the
real world like this, that's the big unlock that will bring on the next 10x
growth in global productivity over the coming decades.

This is a huge market opportunity, and Auki is right at the epicenter of it.

## $AUKI: A Deflationary Burn-and-Mint Token

Auki is building their posemesh network as a DePIN — a collaborative global
network that is both owned and operated by the people, for the people — all
glued together by the $AUKI token.

It's the standard DePIN setup: customers buy access to the network using $AUKI
tokens, and then some of those tokens are used to reward the hardware providers
for their contribution. There's a small twist to it though, because each time a
customer pays for access, some of the tokens are burnt. This creates a so called
deflationary burn-and-mint, which makes the token capture value: the more the
network is used, the more $AUKI will be bought and burnt.

This connection between real-world usage and token demand is exactly what you
want to see in a sustainable token model.
